Introduction

Quality of Experience (QoE) analytics provide information to help understand the user's video streaming experience, including playback success, startup time, rebuffering, and visual quality.

QoE Analytics provide Brightcove customers with a transparent, meaningful, and well-curated set of metrics focused on the following:

  • Troubleshooting: What errors are viewers experiencing? Are errors related to the player, CDN, or encoding? Can I mitigate these errors?
  • Reporting: How is my service performing across key QoE metrics? Are there trends that I should be aware of and address?
  • Monitoring: Tracking near real-time metrics that show how viewers are impacted.

Navigation

  1. Open Video Cloud Studio.
  2. In the primary navigation, click Analytics.
  3. In the panel navigation, click QOE.

Setup

Contact your Customer Success Manager to enable your account for QoE Analytics. This is a paid add-on feature to Video Cloud Studio.

Time range

Click DATE to expand the dropdown menu. Select the time range for your reporting data.

Dimension

Click DIMENSION to expand the dropdown menu. Select the measurement for your reporting data.

Dimensions include:

  • Account (provides overall number)
  • Device
  • Stream type (VOD)
  • Player
  • Country
  • Day

Charts

The following charts trend over time, at a daily or hourly interval (depending on time range). When you select a dimension, it will be reflected in all of the charts.

Error rate

The error rate is the number of errors divided by the number of play requests. The error count includes all error types.

Video start time

The video start time is the average time betweenn play request and stream start.

Stall rate

The stall rate is the total number of stalls divided by the total number of hours streamed.

Upscale time

The upscale time is the average number of seconds per hour of viewing that is spent in an upscaled state (seconds per hour).

Summary table

The date range and dimensions that you set for the page, also applies to the data in the summary table. For example, if you select the Device dimension, you will see a Summary by Device.

Summary by Device

Each column is sortable. The default sort order is video views descending.

Column data:

  • Views - The number of times a video started playing, recorded when the stream is started (does not include rewind or replay); it is not a measure of individual viewers
  • Error count - Number of the errors that prevent or interrupt playback or that could impact start/resume time
  • Error rate - Number of errors that prevent or interrupt playback or that could impact start/resume time divided by the video views for the selected time interval
  • Video start time - Average time between the play request and the stream start per video view; this time does not include ad pre-roll time; start time looks to describe how much time the viewer had to wait before video started to play
  • Video resolution (avg min per hour) - Average minutes watched per hour of viewing, by categories of video resolution (using the height of the video in pixels); weighted by the time watched at that resolution
  • Rebuffering time - Seconds spent rebuffering per hour of watched video
  • Stall rate - Total number of stalls divided by the sum of the watch time
  • Bitrate - the average bitrate played in the player weighted by the duration it was watched
  • Rendition switches - Average number of switches across all sessions
  • Upscale factor - The ratio between player resolution and delivered video resolution when the player viewport is bigger than the delivered resolution
  • Upscale time - The average number of seconds per hour of viewing that is spent in an upscaled state
